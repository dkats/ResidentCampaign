Ideas for My Dream Hospital

  • Decorations EVERYWHERE
    • Photos in the patients' rooms

      "I'm sorry- I hung up pictures so you can see who she is. This isn't her."

      Many times in the ICU I depend on family photos to visualize the person I haven't yet met- free from plastic and tubes and beeps and blares.

      Families, please don't apologize for sharing these moments.

      — Ryan JJ Buckley (@Ryan_jjb) March 2, 2019
    • Letting patients decorate their rooms, keeping them busy, and giving them interesting things to look at

      So that, as they were wheeled about the hospital, there wasn't a place they could look without seeing something positive, uplifting and fun. I've always heard @primarychildren was a top-notch facility, but it became all to clear once you walk through the door. They CARE. pic.twitter.com/4Dbgn0CuAi

      — Jared Jeffery (@Jk_Jeffery) May 3, 2018
  • Documenting my patients' lives

    One of my favorite Pediatrician ‘things’ is documenting my patient’s response to: what do you want to be?
    I get answers ~3yr+ til ~9yrs old.
    At 10yr+, when they don’t answer I read their answers back to them:
    at:
    3 a Mommy
    4 a princess
    5 a dancer
    6 a vet...
    (they always smile!)

    — Dr. Todd Wolynn (@DrToddWo) February 22, 2019
  • Humanizing patients
    • Asking each patient/parent for three descriptive adjectives, and then incorporating them into my notes and presenations to humanize the patients.

    • “Patient endorsed...”
      “Patient complained of...”
      “Patient is a 75 y/o M”
      etc

      drive me bonkers. Use names! https://t.co/PuHsE6BrNs

      — Emily Fridenmaker (@emily_fri) February 9, 2019

    • Today I snuck a patient a whole 6-pack of ginger ale from the supply fridge.

      Another patient, bedbound. I borrowed a wheel chair and raced her down the halls.

      I talked to an agitated patient, instead of using haldol.

      These are the things that make me feel most like a doctor.

      — TJT, M.D. (@TJTra28) September 6, 2018

    • Last pt of the day. 88 yo widower of 1 yr w newly dx AF.
      Crying, he lamented "I'm falling apart, I don't know what to do"
      I hugged him. He said "I haven't had a hug in a yr" & smiled.
      My day was complete. Only real "doctoring" I did all day.#patientcare #humanism

      — David May (@DavidMayMD) September 14, 2018
  • Helping patients survive the deluge of people walking into their rooms

    I tell patients 1) the name of the hospitalist taking over, 2) that they are incredible, and one incredible skill, strength or accomplishment, and 3) something funny about how they look (looks like Clark Kent, tall skinny white guy like me, tiny but mighty, etc.). pic.twitter.com/SiDTpeWWdK

    — Medical Axioms (@medicalaxioms) August 3, 2018
  • Feedback!

    The consultant in clinic gave me a feedback form:

    - Did you feel welcome in this clinic?
    - Do you think you've learnt something today?
    - Would you recommend this clinic to other students?
    - Would you be happy for your relative to be treated by me? If not, why?

    Fantastic doctor.

    — Gemma Wren (@gnwren) February 8, 2019
