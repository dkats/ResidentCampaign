Quotations

Random quotations that mean something to me (in no particular order).

Nobody cares how much you know, until they know how much you care.
— Theodore Roosevelt (allegedly)

I do not believe we can understand our patients' bodies if we do not understand something about who they are as human beings, and what their lives are like.
— Dr. Colleen Farrell, Social History as a Story

Just because your voice now goes all across the world does not mean you are any wiser than when it only went to the end of the bar.
— Edward R. Murrow

Just because you're in this to reduce human suffering, doesn't mean you have to increase your own.
— Dominic Vitello (medical school classmate)

It's easier to be a doctor than to be a patient.
— Dr. Jeffry Katz (medical school clinical diagnosis instructor)

Make tomorrow a little better than today.
— Dr. Brian Skotko (research mentor)

...despite completing my training, despite being surrounded by every form and severity of disease, I had yet to learn what it meant to be sick.
— Dr. Rana Awdish, In Shock (p. 2)

'Every listener hears a different story,' I reminded him. 'The fact that she shares something different with her nurse than what she tells you or me is to be expected,' I explained. I knew she had different answers for different people. We each had different relationships with her. 'It doesn't devalue what she told you, it's just different,' I added.
— Dr. Rana Awdish, In Shock (p. 6)

I knew we valued the cure, the goal, the win. We were far less comfortable in the gray, shadowed area of suffering.
— Dr. Rana Awdish, In Shock (p. 9)

We aren't trained to see our patients. We are trained to see pathology.
— Dr. Rana Awdish, In Shock (p. 26)
